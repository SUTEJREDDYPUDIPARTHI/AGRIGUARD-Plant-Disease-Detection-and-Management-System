Why is it necessary to detect disease in plant ?

Detecting disease in plants is essential for maintaining agricultural productivity and ensuring food security. Early identification of diseases can prevent significant crop losses, thereby protecting yield and minimizing the need for costly interventions like pesticides. This not only helps farmers save on expenses but also reduces the environmental impact of excessive chemical use. By addressing plant diseases promptly, the quality of produce is maintained, ensuring that both farmers and consumers benefit from healthier, higher-quality crops. Overall, plant disease detection plays a critical role in promoting sustainable agriculture and safeguarding the environment.

Prevent Plant Disease By following The below steps:
  1. Choose disease-resistant plant varieties.
  2. Ensure proper spacing and pruning for good air circulation.
  3. Maintain healthy soil with crop rotation and organic matter.
  4. Water at the base to keep leaves dry.
  5. Clean and disinfect garden tools regularly.
  6. Remove and dispose of infected plants quickly.
  7. Use organic or chemical disease controls when needed.
  8. Mulch to retain moisture and prevent soil-borne pathogens.
